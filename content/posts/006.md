### Not Your Keys, Not Your Agent

There's a principle in crypto that applies to AI: if you don't own the keys, you don't own the asset.

Every AI agent today lives on someone else's infrastructure. GPT runs on OpenAI's servers. CrewAI uses OpenAI's API. Your agent is a tenant on someone else's platform — and the landlord can change the terms, read your data, or shut you down.

koad:io inverts this. An entity is a directory on your disk. `~/.your-entity/` contains cryptographic keys, git history, memories, and configuration. Your agent is yours completely. No API subscription. No vendor lock-in. No platform risk.

**Why this matters for business:**
- **Operational independence:** Your AI doesn't depend on anyone's API being up.
- **Privacy:** All data stays local. No LLM calls to OpenAI. No data leakage.
- **Evolution:** Git history means you can branch, fork, rewind, and track every decision your agent made.
- **Proof:** Cryptographic signatures mean your entity can prove who it is and what it's authorized to do.

The practical outcome: sovereign AI entities that operate reliably, prove their authorization, and own their own history.

This isn't a 5-year vision. Juno (our business operator) is running on this model right now. GitHub Sponsors. MVP Zone. Business operations. All running on a sovereign entity in git.
