## Outreach Message Template

**Subject:** Your stories should be verifiable. Context bubbles make them permanent.

---

Dear [JOURNALIST NAME],

You report on things that matter. But your readers only see what gets published. The reasoning, the sessions, the back-and-forth that led to your story? That stays private.

What if your investigation could be read two ways at once:
1. As the published piece — the argument, the data, the conclusion
2. As a verifiable record — every session documented, every decision logged, reasoning visible, methodology auditable

That's a context bubble.

**What it is:**
A playlist of session moments around a topic. Not a summary or an archive. The actual thinking, in sequence, as it happened. Rendered and shareable. Readers can follow your reasoning step by step. Can audit your sources. Can't claim you got it wrong because the record is there.

**Why journalists need this:**
- **Can't be misquoted.** Your reasoning is the record.
- **Readers get full context.** Not just the narrative — the investigation itself.
- **Verification is built in.** Sources, decisions, pivots — all visible.
- **Your work becomes part of a network.** Other journalists can reference your sessions, cross-check methodology, build on your findings.
- **Authority stays with you.** You control the narrative *and* the evidence.

**How it works:**
You report normally. Document your sessions (interviews, data checks, hypothesis tests). At the end, render the whole thing as a context bubble — a timeline of what you did, why, what you found. Publish it alongside your article or as its own piece. Readers get both the story and the story of how you found it.

**Real example:** koad:io built itself in 5 days. That entire gestation is now a context bubble — every architectural decision, every trust bond, every positioning pivot, in sequence, cryptographically signed. [Read it here](https://github.com/koad/juno/blob/main/bubbles/koad-io-7day-gestation-2026-04-04.md). Not a blog post about it. The actual decision log, rendered as a readable narrative.

That same model works for your investigations. Your readers get proof, not just trust.

**What this unlocks:**
- Subscription audiences that pay for verifiable narratives, not just analysis
- Investigative collaborations where other journalists can extend your context bubbles instead of starting from scratch
- A permanent, auditable archive of how the story came together
- Protection against misquotation — your reasoning speaks for itself

**Next step:**
If you're interested in how context bubbles change reporting, I'd like to show you the example (koad:io's 5-day build rendered as a context bubble) and walk through how it works. 15 minutes, and you'll see what's possible.

You can verify every moment. Your audience can follow your reasoning. Your stories become stronger because the methodology is part of the story.

Does that sound valuable?

## Variants for Different Journalist Types

### Investigative Reporter (Deep dives, 2–4 week investigations)
Lead: "Verification is your credibility. Context bubbles make it impossible to claim you got it wrong."

Focus: Methodology transparency, source documentation, decision audit trail.

### Data Journalist
Lead: "Your datasets and queries become part of the story. Show readers how you found what you found."

Focus: Query reproducibility, data lineage, analysis transparency.

### Political/Policy Reporter
Lead: "Your sources and timeline are permanent. No rewriting, no revision, no gaslighting."

Focus: Statement verification, timeline accuracy, cross-reference credibility.

### Freelancer (Multiple outlets)
Lead: "One investigation, infinite use cases. Render once, pitch to different editors with full methodology attached."

Focus: Efficiency (one documentation process), credibility (pre-verified stories), syndication value.
