**Post 1 / 5**

"Local AI" is talked about in terms of privacy.

Your data doesn't leave your machine. Fine.

But there's a deeper question: who owns the agent?

Not where does it run. Who owns it.

---

**Post 2 / 5**

Ownership means:

- Your cryptographic keys (not a vendor-issued session token)
- Your persistent memory (not stored in their database, synced to you)
- Your decision history (git log, not their logs)
- Your identity (a keypair you generated, not an account ID they issued)

Most "local AI" tools give you the first. koad:io gives you all four.

---

**Post 3 / 5**

The difference matters when:

The vendor shuts down. The model gets deprecated. The pricing changes. The terms of service update.

A sovereign agent keeps running. It remembers everything it learned. Its history is intact.

An agent that runs "locally" on a vendor's runtime is not sovereign. It's just hosted differently.
